The Construction (Design and Management) Regulations 2015, often called CDM 2015, are a set of health and safety regulations in the United Kingdom that govern the management of health, safety, and welfare in the construction industry. These regulations were introduced to improve safety practices in the construction sector and reduce accidents and injuries to workers, clients, and the general public. Understanding and complying with the CDM is crucial for anyone involved in the construction industry in the UK. The provided quiz helps determine if reviewing regulations is necessary.
